Methodology & Data Standards

Inclusion criteria: Events are included where there is at least one credible open-source report — an official Ukrainian military or intelligence statement, verified video evidence, or reporting by two or more independent outlets. Russian acknowledgement is noted where it exists but is not required for inclusion.

Damage classification: A consistent taxonomy is applied across all entries. Sunk = total loss, vessel confirmed on seabed. Destroyed = total loss confirmed on land or in air. Damaged = partial damage, target operational or repairable. Shot Down = aerial asset destroyed in flight. Disrupted = infrastructure or operation temporarily put out of action.

Personnel casualty figures: This tracker uses Mediazona/BBC News Russian confirmed-by-name figures as its primary KIA metric — the most rigorous publicly available methodology, cross-checked against Russia's National Probate Registry. These figures are always a floor, not a ceiling. Statistical estimates from Meduza/Mediazona's probate registry model are cited alongside for context.

CASUALTY & LOSS TRACKING
📋
Mediazona
Confirmed KIA · Named list

Independent Russian media outlet maintaining the most rigorous publicly available list of Russian soldiers killed — confirmed by name using obituaries, social media, and official sources. Cross-checked against the National Probate Registry. This tracker uses Mediazona/BBC as its primary KIA source.

en.zona.media ↗
📊
Meduza
Statistical KIA estimates

Independent Russian investigative outlet. Works with Mediazona to produce statistical estimates of total Russian deaths using probate registry excess mortality data — giving a broader picture beyond what's confirmed by name. Estimated ~220,000 killed by Aug 2025.

meduza.io ↗
🔍
Oryx
Photo-confirmed equipment losses

The gold standard for equipment loss tracking — every entry in the Oryx database is individually photo or video confirmed. Tracks Russian and Ukrainian losses of tanks, AFVs, aircraft, and naval vessels. Ceased updating in 2025 but the historical record remains essential.

oryxspioenkop.com ↗
NAVAL & UNMANNED SYSTEMS ANALYSIS
⚓
Covert Shores
USV / naval analysis · H.I. Sutton

The most detailed open-source technical analysis of Ukrainian and Russian USVs. H.I. Sutton documents every known drone boat variant with specifications, operational history, and imagery. Essential reference for USV platform identification and capabilities.

hisutton.com ↗
✈
The War Zone (TWZ)
Defence reporting · OSINT synthesis

Consistently the best English-language specialist defence reporting on USV and UGV developments. Well-sourced, sceptical of unverified claims, and fast to cover new platform developments. Used extensively as a secondary verification source for this tracker.

twz.com ↗
🇺🇦
Militarnyi
Ukrainian defence · weapons & systems

Ukrainian-language defence publication with strong English coverage of weapons systems, unmanned platforms and battlefield technology. Particularly valuable for early reporting on new Ukrainian drone and UGV developments, often sourced directly from manufacturers and the Ministry of Defence.

militarnyi.com ↗
📊
Petro Ivaniuk Dataset
Air defence data · Kaggle / GitHub

Ukrainian data scientist maintaining the most comprehensive structured dataset of Russian drone and missile attacks on Ukraine, compiled daily from official Ukrainian Air Force reports. Verified by CSIS as matching official figures. Covers every attack wave from October 2022 to present — the primary data source for the Air Defence tab on this tracker.

github.com/PetroIvaniuk ↗
🎯
Tochnyi
Strike analysis · Air defence attrition research

Ukrainian open-source research publication producing detailed long-form analysis of Ukraine's strike campaigns against Russian military assets. Primary source for the Air Defence Attrition section in this tracker — their March 2026 investigation documents Ukraine's systematic campaign against Russian SAM systems and radar networks across Crimea and occupied territories from 2025 onwards.

tochnyi.info ↗
GROUND WARFARE & UGV ANALYSIS
🔬
Jamestown Foundation
UGV analysis · Eurasia security

Publishes rigorous analytical pieces on Ukrainian and Russian UGV developments, including the landmark piece on Ukraine's emergence as a world leader in unmanned ground vehicles. Good source for doctrine-level analysis beyond individual incidents.

jamestown.org ↗
🏛
Modern War Institute (West Point)
Academic · operational analysis

West Point's MWI publishes practitioner-level analysis on UGV employment in Ukraine, including detailed pieces on networked ground robot operations and lessons for Western militaries. Peer-reviewed and operationally grounded.

mwi.westpoint.edu ↗
🇺🇦
Kyiv Independent
Ukrainian news · frontline reporting

Leading English-language Ukrainian outlet with reliable frontline reporting on drone and UGV deployment. Close access to Ukrainian military sources makes it particularly useful for first-disclosure reporting on new systems and operations.

kyivindependent.com ↗
🏛
CSIS — Futures Lab
Strategic analysis · equipment data

Center for Strategic and International Studies publishes detailed quantitative analysis of Russian equipment losses and battlefield dynamics, including the Russian Firepower Strike Tracker and the landmark "Russia's Battlefield Woes" analysis (2025).

csis.org ↗
LIVE CONFLICT TRACKING TOOLS
🗺
DeepState Map
Live frontline · territorial control

Real-time OSINT map of frontline positions and territorial control, maintained by a Ukrainian analytical team. Considered one of the most accurate live maps of the conflict. Updated continuously based on visual evidence and geolocated footage.

deepstatemap.live ↗
📍
Geoconfirmed
Geolocated OSINT incidents

Crowdsourced OSINT initiative that geolocates conflict events — missile strikes, troop movements, infrastructure damage. Each entry is individually geolocated and verified. Good for cross-referencing specific incident locations.

geoconfirmed.azurewebsites.net ↗
👁
Eyes on Russia
Verified incidents · war crimes documentation

Run by the Centre for Information Resilience with hundreds of volunteers. Tracks attacks and incidents, labelling each as verified or unverified. Particularly strong on human rights documentation and civilian harm but also covers military incidents.

eyesonrussia.org ↗
📡
Institute for the Study of War (ISW)
Daily operational updates

Publishes daily updates on battlefield developments with map assessments. Widely cited by Western governments and media. Useful for operational context and frontline movement analysis, though assessments should be cross-referenced with other sources.

understandingwar.org ↗

ABOUT THIS TRACKER
About

This tracker is an independent open-source research project documenting the confirmed and claimed use of unmanned systems — surface, ground, and air — in the Russian invasion of Ukraine.

It is built and maintained as part of ongoing PhD research in war studies, drawing on publicly available OSINT, official Ukrainian military releases, and verified reporting. All entries carry source citations for independent verification.

Data is updated regularly. The tracker is not affiliated with any government, military organisation, or commercial entity.
